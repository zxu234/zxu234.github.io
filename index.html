About me:

My undergraduate studies were in forestry, environmental studies, and Japanese language. Thus, my projects as a cartographer mainly focus on natural resources related topics and East-Asian cultures.

I used to work as forester for a private consulting company with branches in the Great Lake states and for the Wisconsin Department of Natural Resources. During my works as a forester I was often tasked to make forest stands navigation map for the team for data gathering, so my map designing method tends to be practical and as less distracting as possible. Generally I would avoid using too many colors to keep my projects clean.

My career ineretsts are about using my cartohrapher and forester skills to design maps and help field workers navigate through tough terrains in forestry industries, making demographic info maps for researchers or companies whose markets are in East-Asia and help them guide their marketing strategies, and designing interactive maps for public health department as the past year of pandemic had let me realize that how GIS and cartography skills could be combined to serve as an important tool to save lives.
